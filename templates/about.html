About DeepTrace

We propose a generic framework for time-series forecasting called DeepTrace, which com- prises three main task-specific components - Convolutional Block, Recurrent Block, and Linear Block. The framework involves the use of these components in a specific order and is defined for certain permu- tations, described as architectural variants in the paper. We also introduce a novel training methodology of using future contextual frames to train the framework. The genericness of the framework is tested by evaluating the performance on real-world time series datasets across diverse domains. The future con- textual frames are dropped during the testing phase to simulate the real-world conditions, which helps us verify the robustness of DeepTrace in real-world scenarios. We use an optimizer to offset the loss incurred due to the non-provision of future contextual frames. We conducted substantial experiments by applying the proposed framework across the datasets considered and comparing the performance to benchmark results for those datasets. These experiments show the effectiveness of the proposed frame- work that outperforms the existing state-of-art methods.
